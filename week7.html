Week Seven Reflection

1. What went well in the design process?

One aspect that did go pretty well this week was how smoothly the coding process went once I finally started. I think I've gotten more used to coding now, and I understand what I'm working on much more compared to before. This improvement helped make the coding process feel a little less daunting [for future coding days to come], and more manageable.

2. Did you accomplish your sprint goal? Why/Why not?

Truthfully, one of the challenges I faced was the unwillingness to start coding the app. I felt like I didn't know enough to start, which led me to focusing on other work. I found it ironic that when I actually started, it was less difficult than I made it out to be. This delay of course, shortened the timeline I had to work on the code, so it's frustrating that this really could have been avoided, had I just gotten over the worry and just started. Additionally to this, I intended to create a detailed daily work plan to help guide my progress, but I did not follow through with it over last weekend, causing this weeks workflow unstructured and unorganized.

3. What didn't go well in the design process?

A key area that I constantly need to work on is task management and prioritization. I keep focusing my time on other parts of the project, mainly as a way to avoid working on the coding aspect of the project. I've learned that I know more than I realize, so it was just wasteful to push these tasks away, as confronting them earlier would have lead to a more productive work week.

4. What can be improved? What lessons have you learnt?

Starting the topic searching, I just chose the topics that I usually go for [which is environmental regeneration and arts and cultural expression, but I don't really do civic participation, just chose this to try out something new] and from there I was just stuck, since these are topics I have overdone in the past. Going back and actually brainstorming personal daily challenges helped me choose a topic that I would relate to. What I have learnt from this was being more thorough with brainstorming really helped set up for choosing a topic I would be able to empathise with.

5. What actions will I take for the next sprint?

For the final sprint, I plan to implement a more structured and intentional schedule. Specifically, I will organize a daily [potentially an hourly?] checklist to help break my project schedule into a manageable and realistic timeline, trying to make sure that I give an equal amount of attention across the collateral.
