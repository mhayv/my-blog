Week One Reflection

1. What went well in the design process?

What went well this week was choosing and refining a topic and problem statement. I was quite stuck in choosing a topic from the list of areas of inquiry to consider, so I went back and fully brainstormed what challenges I face in my daily life. That really helped me choose something that I would actually be interested in, since I am also affected by this problem. I also took a different approach from last term, by writing out my planning and idea refining on my iPad, instead of just typing on my laptop. This felt more free, and helped me to write down my thoughts more openly. This helped a lot when I was refining my current problem statement.

2. Did you accomplish your sprint goal? Why/Why not?

My sprint goal was to choose a topic and to have a starting problem statement [next week is ideally, setting an actionable problem statement], and I have achieved this through exploring potential topics, narrowing in on certain ideas within a chosen topic [civic participation -> local events participation], and then refining the problem statement.

3. What didn't go well in the design process?

It took longer than I would have liked to settle on a topic that I wanted to explore. Up until Thursday [after mentoring], I was doing research on two potential topics 'civic participation', and 'environmental regeneration', so that took away the time I could have spent focusng on just one topic [if I had just chosen something from the get go]. I think part of the hold back [on choosing a topic] was because of being unsure if my research was actually aligning with the brief.

4. What can be improved? What lessons have you learnt?

Starting the topic searching, I just chose the topics that I usually go for [which is environmental regeneration and arts and cultural expression, but I don't really do civic participation, just chose this to try out something new] and from there I was just stuck, since these are topics I have overdone in the past. Going back and actually brainstorming personal daily challenges helped me choose a topic that I would relate to. What I have learnt from this was being more thorough with brainstorming really helped set up for choosing a topic I would be able to empathise with.

5. What actions will I take for the next sprint?

For the following sprint, I will set up an actionable problem statement [potentially a 'How Might We'], start UX research and interviews, and after gaining insights on the interview results, mapping out wireframes and current user journeys.
